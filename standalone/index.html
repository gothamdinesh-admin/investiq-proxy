Help & FAQ

How do I import my eToro / Sharesies portfolio?
eToro: Portfolio → ⋯ menu → Export → CSV
Sharesies: Account → Investing activity → Download CSV
Hatch: Account → Statements → CSV (or use Akahu sync if available)
Then go to Holdings tab → Upload CSV. The importer auto-detects column names (works with "Open Rate", "purchasePrice", "buyPrice", etc).
What does "Snapshot date" mean on import?
The date stamp recorded with this import. Defaults to today. Set it to a past date to backfill your performance history (e.g. import a CSV you exported 3 months ago and tag it with that date — your performance chart gets a data point there).
Why is my BTC / ETH price so low?
Yahoo Finance uses different ticker symbols for crypto. We auto-fix common ones on every load (BTC → BTC-USD, ETH → ETH-USD). If a price still looks wrong, run Admin Panel → Fix Crypto Tickers manually.
Why does my UK stock (e.g. AZN.L) show a strange return?
London Stock Exchange tickers (.L suffix) are quoted in pence, but most users enter purchase price in pounds. We auto-divide by 100 for any .L ticker. If your CSV had price in pence, you may need to multiply by 100 in your CSV before importing.
What's "Ignore" on a holding?
Mark a holding as ignored to keep it visible but exclude it from totals (value, cost, return %, weight). Useful for cash accounts, dormant positions, or ANZ/BNZ bank balances synced via Akahu that shouldn't pollute your investment-return number.
How does cloud sync work?
Your portfolio syncs to Supabase 2 seconds after any change. Open the app on another device, sign in, and your data appears. The pill at the top of the header shows the last sync time — click it to force-pull from cloud now.
What do the AI agents do? Are they giving me financial advice?
Four specialised Claude agents analyse your portfolio in parallel:
• Portfolio Health — diversification + concentration scoring
• Market Impact — global factors affecting your holdings
• Opportunity Scout — gaps and underweight sectors
• Senior Advisor — synthesises into a 5-point action plan
This is analysis only, NOT personalised financial advice. Talk to a licensed adviser before making decisions.
What is FIF and when does it apply?
NZ's Foreign Investment Fund tax. If the original cost of your overseas (non-NZ) holdings exceeds NZ$50,000 at any point in the year, you owe FIF income tax. The NZ Tax (FIF) tab shows your current overseas cost basis and a Fair Dividend Rate (5%) estimate. Always confirm with an accountant before filing.
Where's my data stored? Is it secure?
• Browser: localStorage (encrypted at rest by your OS)
• Cloud: Supabase Postgres with Row-Level Security — only you can read/write your own data
• API keys: never sync to cloud, stay in your browser only
• Sessions: auto-logout after 30 minutes of inactivity
• Proxy: rate-limited and origin-locked
How do I see my activity history?
Every login, CSV import, holding change, and AI agent run is logged. Admins can see all users' activity in Admin Panel → Activity Log. Regular-user-facing audit view coming soon.
How do I delete my account / data?
Settings → Danger Zone → Clear All Data wipes both browser AND cloud. To fully delete your auth account, ask an admin to remove you from Supabase Authentication.

NZ Foreign Investment Fund (FIF) Check

NZ residents: if the original cost of your overseas investments exceeds NZD $50,000 at any time during the year, you generally owe FIF income tax. This is analysis only, not tax advice — talk to an accountant.

Method Optimiser IQ

Lower

Note: CV here approximates the year's gain as market value − cost basis. The exact IRD CV formula uses opening market value + purchases during the year. For a buy-and-hold portfolio these are close, but confirm with your accountant before filing — especially if you bought or sold mid-year.
Overseas Holdings Contributing to FIF
About the FIF rules
• The NZ$50,000 threshold is based on original cost of overseas investments (not current market value).
• NZ-listed, ASX-listed (some), and AU-unit-trust holdings are generally exempt.
• Two main calculation methods: FDR (Fair Dividend Rate, 5% flat) and CV (Comparative Value, capped at actual gain).
• InvestIQ shows the FDR estimate as the default conservative view. Your actual liability may be lower under CV.
• Always consult a qualified NZ tax adviser before filing.
