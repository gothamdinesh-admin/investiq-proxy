About the Hub

One view of your wealth.
Built for the way New Zealanders actually invest.

Most portfolio tools were built somewhere else — for someone else. They tag your Harbour fund as "international", forget PIE tax exists, and quietly miss your FIF threshold.

Harbour Investor Hub is different. It assumes you live in Auckland, hold a Harbour fund, contribute to KiwiSaver, run a Sharesies account on the side, and pay NZ tax under either FIF or PIE. It does the maths for you.

Whole-of-life view

Harbour funds, KiwiSaver, eToro, Sharesies, ANZ, BNZ, term deposits — consolidated in one dashboard.

NZ-tax aware

FIF threshold tracking, FDR vs CV comparison, PIE treatment for NZ funds, IR3-ready exports.

AI-guided

A four-agent AI panel (Claude Haiku + Opus) gives daily briefings and per-holding context — never advice, always information.

Why it matters to you

01

Your Harbour funds, kept current

Unit prices for every Harbour fund — Australasian Equity, Income, Active Growth, the lot — refreshed daily and shown alongside the rest of your wealth. No more checking the fund-update email when you want a portfolio snapshot.

02

PIE tax handled separately

Your Harbour fund income is taxed via PIE — your provider reports to IRD on your behalf. The Hub keeps it visually separated from FIF-zone holdings so you can see your true after-tax position at a glance.

03

See your full NZ equity exposure

Hold a Harbour Australasian fund and Smartshares NZ Top 50? The Hub shows your total NZ-equity weighting — Mainfreight, F&P Healthcare, Auckland International — across both. No more wondering if you're double-up.

04

Harbour commentary, at the moment of decision

The AI assistant has Harbour's published research and house views built into its context. When you ask "should I add to NZ utilities?", the answer references Harbour commentary — not random Reddit threads.

05

Concentration & rebalance alerts

The Hub watches your fund weightings and pings you when a holding crosses thresholds — concentration limits, rebalance triggers, or when a Harbour fund drifts above your target allocation.

06

A household view — not just yours

Your partner's KiwiSaver, your kids' custodial accounts, your shared term deposit — the Hub rolls them into one household picture. You decide who sees what.

Tax & Compliance

Built around NZ tax — not bolted on.

FIF, PIE, RWT, imputation credits, foreign withholding — all the things that send your accountant a long email at year-end. The Hub keeps them tidy as you go.

  • FIF threshold tracking — automatic NZ$50k overseas check, per person, with a warning before year-end.
  • FDR vs Comparative Value — both methods calculated, the better outcome flagged.
  • PIE separation — Harbour and other NZ funds are correctly excluded from the FIF calculation.
  • Dividend ledger — RWT, NRWT, imputation, foreign withholding — every credit tracked, IR3-ready.
  • FMA-aligned wording — the Hub provides information, not financial advice. Always.
  • Privacy Act 2020 — data classification audited; no third-party sharing of identified holdings.

FAQ

Questions, honestly answered.

Is the Hub giving me financial advice?

No. The Hub provides information about your own holdings and the market — never personalised financial advice. The AI agents say "consider", "explore", "you may wish to" — never "buy" or "sell". For advice tailored to your circumstances, speak to a licensed financial adviser.

Does Harbour see my non-Harbour holdings?

Identified holdings? Never. Row-level security ensures your portfolio is invisible to Harbour staff. Anonymised, aggregated trends (e.g. "X% of Hub users hold KiwiSaver outside Harbour") may inform Harbour product decisions — and you can opt out of even that.

What happens to my data if I stop using the Hub?

You can export every byte as JSON or CSV, then delete your account in one click. We keep backups for 30 days (for restore purposes only), then they're irrevocably purged. Harbour's record-keeping is unaffected — that data lives with Harbour, as it always did.

How does the FIF threshold check actually work?

The Hub tracks the cost of every overseas holding (excluding Australian shares treated as NZ resident, and PIE funds). It sums them per person — your spouse's are tracked separately — and flags when you cross the NZ$50,000 threshold. From there, it runs both the FDR (5% method) and CV (actual gain) calculations and shows you the better outcome.

Can I use it without being a Harbour client?

Yes — the underlying product is InvestIQ and it's available to any New Zealand investor. The Hub is a partnership flavour for Harbour clients, with Harbour-specific pricing, Harbour commentary built into AI context, and a Harbour-branded experience. Non-Harbour clients access the same platform under the InvestIQ name.

What about my KiwiSaver — does that get tracked too?

Yes. Whether your KiwiSaver is with Harbour, Milford, Generate, Booster, ANZ, ASB, Fisher, or any of the 18 major schemes — the Hub tracks unit prices and balances. It's treated as a PIE for tax purposes, just like a Harbour fund.

What does the AI know about Harbour funds?

For Harbour clients, the AI agents are loaded with Harbour's published research, fund objectives, and house views as context (subject to partnership agreement). When you ask "what's driving my Harbour Australasian fund this quarter?", you get a Harbour-informed answer — not a generic search result.
